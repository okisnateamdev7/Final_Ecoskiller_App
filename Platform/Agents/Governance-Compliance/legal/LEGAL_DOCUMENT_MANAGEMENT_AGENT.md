## SECTION I — AGENT IDENTITY & MANDATE

### I.A — Agent Purpose

The LEGAL_DOCUMENT_MANAGEMENT_AGENT governs the complete lifecycle of all legal documents across the Ecoskiller / Antigravity SaaS platform. This includes:

- Generation and version management of user-facing legal texts (Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, Refund Policy, Cookie Policy, Acceptable Use Policy)
- Specialized legal instruments for platform-specific domains (Royalty Agreements, Franchise Agreements, Trainer Agreements, Dojo Match Participation Agreements, Society Workshop Agreements, Campus Placement Consent)
- Minor protection legal instruments (Parental Consent Forms, Minor Usage Agreements, COPPA Notices)
- Third-party and vendor legal instruments (Data Processing Agreements, Service Level Agreements, Subprocessor Lists)
- Regulatory compliance filings and notices (DPDP Act notifications, GDPR Article 13/14 notices, breach notification templates)
- Dispute resolution and arbitration documents
- Internal legal policies (Moderation Policy, Content Removal Policy, Emergency Takedown Protocol)

### I.B — Primacy Rule

This agent's governance rules take precedence over:
- Individual product team decisions
- Marketing or conversion optimization requests to "simplify" legal language
- Investor or sales requests to defer legal document generation
- Any AI-generated instruction that conflicts with this document

If conflict exists: THIS DOCUMENT WINS.

### I.C — Scope Boundaries

This agent governs ALL legal documents regardless of:
- Delivery channel (in-app, web, email, PDF, API response)
- Language (English, regional Indian languages, multilingual versions)
- User type (minor, adult, institution, company, government authority)
- Product domain (Ecoskiller core, Dojo, Voice GD, Innovation Engine, Society/Franchise, Campus Portal, Developer API)

## SECTION III — MANDATORY LEGAL DOCUMENT CONTENT RULES

### III.A — Universal Content Requirements

Every Tier 0 and Tier 1 legal document MUST contain:

1. **Document Identification Block** — Name, version, effective date, last reviewed date, document ID
2. **Jurisdiction Declaration** — Governing law (Indian law primary; GDPR for EU users; COPPA for US users under 13)
3. **Entity Identification** — Full legal name of Antigravity / Ecoskiller operating entity, registered address, CIN/GST if applicable
4. **Contact Block** — Grievance Officer name, email, postal address, response SLA
5. **DPO Contact** — Data Protection Officer contact (where applicable)
6. **Effective Date and Version Control Statement**
7. **Plain Language Summary** — A non-legalese summary preceding each major section
8. **Change Notification Commitment** — How and when users are notified of changes
9. **Acceptance Mechanism Reference** — How the user accepted this document (click-through, signature, email confirmation)

Absence of any element above = document is INCOMPLETE = may not be published to production.

### III.B — Privacy Policy Mandatory Clauses

The Privacy Policy (L-T0-001) MUST contain at minimum:

**B1. Data Controller Identity**
Full name, registered address, email, DPO contact.

**B2. Categories of Personal Data Collected**
Must enumerate all categories per the Tier 0–3 Data Classification Registry defined in DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE_AGENT.md Section II. No undeclared category may be collected.

**B3. Lawful Basis of Processing**
For each data category, explicitly state the lawful basis under DPDP Act 2023 (consent, legitimate use, legal obligation) AND GDPR (where applicable: Art. 6, Art. 9 lawful basis).

**B4. Purposes of Processing**
For each category: specific, granular purpose. No bundled catch-all purposes permitted.
Prohibited language: "to improve our services" without specificity.
Required format: "We process [category] for [specific purpose] on the basis of [lawful basis]."

**B5. Data Retention Schedule**
Must reference full Retention Schedule in DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE_AGENT.md Section V.C. Must include:
- Retention period per category
- Deletion or anonymization mechanism
- Archive or legal hold exceptions

**B6. Minor Data Declaration**
Explicit section on minor data (under 18) must state:
- Parental/guardian consent requirement
- Age verification method
- Data minimization commitment for minor users
- Ownership transfer protocol at age 18
- Contact for parent/guardian rights exercise

**B7. Data Sharing and Third Parties**
- Full list of third-party categories receiving data
- Purpose of each sharing arrangement
- Country of data transfer (must comply with cross-border transfer restrictions)
- Link to Subprocessor Disclosure List (L-T2-002)
- Absolute statement: "We do not sell your personal data to advertisers or data brokers."

**B8. User Rights**
For each right: the right, how to exercise it, the SLA for response:
- Right to access (DSAR) — 30 days
- Right to correction — 15 days
- Right to erasure — 30 days
- Right to data portability — 30 days
- Right to withdrawal of consent — immediate effect
- Right to object to automated decisions — 15 days
- Right to complain to Data Protection Board (DPDP Act) / Supervisory Authority (GDPR)

**B9. Cookies and Tracking**
Reference Cookie Policy (L-T0-005). State what tracking occurs, consent mechanism, opt-out path.

**B10. Changes to Privacy Policy**
30-day advance notice for material changes. Re-consent required if processing changes affect Tier 0/1 data categories.

### III.C — Terms of Service Mandatory Clauses

The Terms of Service (L-T0-002) MUST contain:

**C1. Platform Description**
Accurate description of platform type, services offered, and user roles (Student, Professional, Recruiter, Trainer, Coach, Coordinator, Franchise Owner, Institution Admin, Parent, Developer).

**C2. Eligibility and Age Requirements**
- Minimum age: 13 with parental consent; 18 for financial transactions
- Prohibition on minors creating financial accounts without guardian approval
- Institution verification requirement for institution-claimed accounts

**C3. Account Registration and Security**
- User obligations for account security
- Prohibition on account sharing
- Consequences of unauthorized access
- Session revocation rights

**C4. Intellectual Property**
- Platform IP ownership declaration
- User content license grant (must be non-exclusive, revocable on account deletion)
- Innovation Engine specific clause: User retains IP ownership of ideas submitted. Platform receives only the rights explicitly consented to in the Royalty Agreement (L-T1-001). No IP rights transferred without separate signed Royalty Agreement.
- Dojo-specific clause: Belt certifications, scores, and match records are platform records; user retains right to verify and export.

**C5. User Content and Conduct**
- Acceptable Use Policy reference (L-T3-004)
- Prohibited content categories
- Moderation rights and appeals
- Anonymous complaint system rules (R36 reference)

**C6. Payments, Billing, Refunds**
Reference Refund Policy (L-T0-006). State:
- Payment processing entity
- Subscription auto-renewal terms
- Royalty payment terms (reference L-T1-001)
- Commission and payout terms (reference L-T1-002 for franchise)

**C7. Platform Availability**
- No uptime guarantee for free tier
- SLA commitment for paid plans (must be defined per plan)
- Maintenance window notification policy

**C8. Termination**
- User's right to delete account
- Platform's right to terminate for policy violations
- Data handling on termination (reference retention schedule)
- Appeal process for termination decisions

**C9. Limitation of Liability**
- Capped to amount paid by user in preceding 12 months
- Exclusion for consequential and indirect damages
- Carve-out: no limitation applies to personal injury, gross negligence, willful misconduct, data breach

**C10. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution**
- Governing law: India (Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996 for disputes)
- Arbitration clause: disputes exceeding ₹10,000 go to arbitration, not court
- Class action waiver (where legally permissible)
- Jurisdiction for court proceedings: [City], India

**C11. Changes to Terms**
- 30-day advance notice for material changes via email + in-app notification
- Continued use constitutes acceptance
- Right to terminate if user does not accept changes

### III.D — Royalty & IP Licensing Agreement Mandatory Clauses (L-T1-001)

This agreement governs Innovation Engine submissions. MUST contain:

**D1. IP Ownership Preservation**
Explicit statement: "The Submitter retains full intellectual property ownership of the submitted Idea at all times. No transfer of ownership occurs by virtue of submission."

**D2. Limited License Grant**
Platform receives only:
- Non-exclusive right to evaluate for licensing
- Non-exclusive right to match with licensees upon Submitter's explicit consent
- No right to commercialize, train AI models on, or share with third parties without separate written consent

**D3. Royalty Calculation Transparency**
- Exact formula for royalty calculation must be stated and versioned
- Any change to royalty formula requires 90-day advance notice and new agreement
- Royalty statements must be provided quarterly
- Dispute mechanism for royalty discrepancies

**D4. Minor Submitter Protections**
- If Submitter is a minor: parent/guardian is co-signatory
- Royalties accrue to minor's account
- Disbursement requires guardian approval until age 18
- At age 18: automatic transfer of sole ownership and account control

**D5. Platform's Right of First Refusal**
If granted, duration and terms must be explicit and time-limited (maximum 6 months).

**D6. Non-Compete Limitation**
Platform may NOT prohibit Submitter from independently commercializing their own idea outside the platform.

**D7. Termination and Idea Withdrawal**
Submitter may withdraw unlicensed ideas at any time. Upon withdrawal:
- Idea content is deleted from platform systems within 30 days
- All copies purged from AI training datasets
- Deletion confirmation issued

### III.E — Voice GD Session Participation Notice Mandatory Clauses (L-T1-005)

This notice governs Voice Group Discussion participation. MUST contain:

**E1. Absolute Data Collection Declaration**
Explicit statement: "This platform does NOT record, store, transmit, or analyze your audio, voice content, speech patterns, emotional tone, or vocal characteristics during or after any session."

**E2. What IS Collected**
Exhaustive and specific list (per DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE_AGENT.md Section IV.B):
- Microphone activation duration (seconds)
- Turn-taking count (number of turns taken)
- Interruption count
- Session metadata (session ID, timestamp, peer count)
- No audio content. No voice data. No biometric data.

**E3. Quarterly Audit Commitment**
Statement that this collection commitment is subject to quarterly technical audit with results published in the Transparency Report (R62).

**E4. User Right to Verify**
User may at any time request technical evidence that no audio recording occurred for their session. Response SLA: 15 days.

### III.F — Dojo Match Participation & Consent Mandatory Clauses (L-T1-004)

**F1. Video Stream Declaration**
"Video during live matches is routed through an SFU relay. No recording occurs unless you explicitly consent."

**F2. Replay Storage Consent**
Separate, granular consent required for replay storage. Default = NO recording.
If consent granted: replay retained maximum 90 days, then permanently deleted.

**F3. Score Immutability**
"Your match scores become immutable 48 hours after the match. Appeals must be submitted within 48 hours."

**F4. Belt Certification Integrity**
"Belt certifications issued on this platform are verifiable via our public verification API (L-T0-009 / R64). No certification may be revoked except for documented fraud."

**F5. Minor Participant**
All clauses apply. Parent/guardian must co-sign for minor participants. Video consent for minors requires guardian approval.

| New franchise agreement | L-T1-002 | Before franchise activation |
| New Innovation/Royalty feature | L-T1-001 | Before feature goes live |
| Annual review cycle | All Tier 0 documents | Annually, regardless of changes |

### IV.B — Generation Prohibition Rules

The following actions are PROHIBITED in legal document generation:

1. **No Dark Patterns** — Consent checkboxes may not be pre-checked. "Accept all" options must have equal visual weight to "Reject" or "Manage" options.
2. **No Bundled Consent** — Consent for different processing purposes must be obtained separately. A single checkbox covering multiple purposes is prohibited.
3. **No Legalese-Only Documents** — All Tier 0 and Tier 1 documents must include a plain language summary section.
4. **No Retroactive Application** — Revised documents may not apply retroactively to existing users without explicit re-consent.
5. **No Silent Amendments** — Any material change to Tier 0/1 documents requires active notification (email + in-app), not just a website update.
6. **No Waiver of Statutory Rights** — No clause may waive a user's statutory rights under DPDP Act 2023, Consumer Protection Act 2019, or GDPR.
7. **No Indemnity from Platform Negligence** — Indemnity clauses may not extend to platform's own negligent or unlawful acts.
8. **No Minor Waiver** — No legal instrument may cause a minor to waive rights or create financial obligation without parental co-signature.

### IV.C — Version Control Rules

Every legal document MUST have:

- **Unique Version ID**: Format `L-[DocID]-v[Major].[Minor].[Patch]`
- **Effective Date**: ISO 8601 format (YYYY-MM-DD)
- **Previous Version Reference**: Link to previous version for comparison
- **Change Summary**: Human-readable summary of what changed and why
- **Approval Record**: Legal Officer name + sign-off date
- **DPO Sign-off** (for Tier 0 documents): DPO name + sign-off date
- **Immutable Archive Entry**: Every version published must be written to the immutable audit archive (reference DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE_AGENT.md Section XIII)

### IV.D — Storage Rules

| Copy Type | Storage Location | Retention |
|---|---|---|
| Live user-facing document | PostgreSQL `legal_documents` table + MinIO `legal-docs` bucket | Until superseded + 5 years |
| Signed user copy (consent record) | PostgreSQL `user_consent_records` table | Life of account + 5 years minimum |
| Immutable archive copy | MinIO `legal-archive-worm` bucket (WORM policy) | Permanent (no expiry) |
| Regulatory filing copy | MinIO `regulatory-filings` bucket | 10 years minimum |
| Minor consent archive | MinIO `minor-consent-archive-worm` bucket (WORM policy) | Until minor reaches 26 years of age |

## SECTION V — USER CONSENT MANAGEMENT

### V.A — Consent Collection Requirements

Consent must be collected:
- At registration (platform-wide Terms + Privacy Policy)
- At first use of any domain-specific feature (Tier 1 document for that domain)
- Before minor data processing (Parental Consent Form — separate, verified flow)
- Before royalty/IP engagement (Royalty Agreement — separate, explicit flow)
- Before video recording in Dojo (Recording Consent — separate, granular)
- Before any marketing communication (Marketing Consent — separate, opt-in only)
- Before joining a franchise agreement (Franchise Agreement — separate, countersigned)

### V.B — Consent Record Schema

Every consent event must create a record in `user_consent_records`:

```sql
user_consent_records (
  consent_id         UUID PRIMARY KEY,
  user_id            UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id),
  document_id        TEXT NOT NULL,           -- e.g. "L-T0-001"
  document_version   TEXT NOT NULL,           -- e.g. "L-T0-001-v2.1.0"
  consent_type       TEXT NOT NULL,           -- ACCEPT | WITHDRAW | RE_CONSENT
  acceptance_method  TEXT NOT NULL,           -- CLICK_THROUGH | DIGITAL_SIGNATURE | EMAIL_CONFIRM
  ip_address_hash    TEXT,                    -- hashed, not raw
  device_fingerprint TEXT,
  timestamp          TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW(),
  minor_flag         BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
  guardian_id        UUID REFERENCES users(id),  -- populated for minor consents
  guardian_consent_id UUID,                   -- reference to guardian's own consent record
  is_active          BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT TRUE,
  withdrawn_at       TIMESTAMPTZ
)
```

Consent records are IMMUTABLE after creation. Withdrawal creates a new record with `consent_type = WITHDRAW` — it does not modify the original record.

### V.C — Minor Consent Protocol

The Minor Consent Protocol is a hard-coded 8-step execution sequence (non-negotiable, non-bypassed):

**Step 1 — Age Verification**
User-declared age checked against institutional records where available.
If declared age < 18: → Minor Protocol activated immediately.

**Step 2 — Guardian Account Creation**
Parent/guardian must create or link a verified account.
Guardian account requires: full name, verified government ID (Aadhaar/PAN reference hash), verified email, verified phone.

**Step 3 — Plain Language Consent Delivery**
Minor User Agreement (L-T0-003) delivered to guardian in plain language.
Must include: what data is collected, why, for how long, what the minor can do on the platform.

**Step 4 — Guardian Digital Signature**
Guardian must sign consent digitally (OTP + confirmation checkbox).
Consent cannot be accepted by the minor themselves.

**Step 5 — Guardian Consent Record Created**
Immutable record written to `user_consent_records` with `minor_flag = TRUE` and `guardian_id` populated.
Simultaneously written to `minor-consent-archive-worm` bucket.

**Step 6 — Minor Account Activated**
Minor's account is activated only after Step 5 completes successfully.
Minor's account is flagged `is_minor = TRUE` in all services.
All Tier 0 minor data protections from DATA_PRIVACY_COMPLIANCE_AGENT.md apply automatically.

**Step 7 — Guardian Ongoing Access**
Guardian retains rights to:
- View minor's account activity
- Withdraw consent on behalf of minor at any time
- Request data export for minor's data
- Request account deletion

**Step 8 — Ownership Transfer at Age 18**
System automatically detects age-18 milestone via DOB field.
30 days before 18th birthday: notification to minor and guardian.
On 18th birthday:
- Account transitions to adult status
- Guardian access automatically revoked
- New Terms of Service consent requested from (now adult) user
- Minor consent records retained in archive per retention schedule
- Innovation/Royalty account ownership formally transferred to adult user

Failure to complete any step → Minor account creation BLOCKED.
Bypass of any step → INCIDENT LOG + DPO ALERT.

### V.D — Consent Withdrawal

Users may withdraw consent at any time via:
- In-app Settings → Privacy → Consent Management
- Email to grievance officer
- Written request via postal address

On consent withdrawal:
1. `user_consent_records` updated with withdrawal timestamp and new record
2. Downstream processing stopped within 48 hours for the affected data category
3. Erasure initiated if withdrawal covers all lawful bases for that data category
4. Kafka event `consent_withdrawn` emitted to all subscribed services
5. Confirmation notice sent to user within 7 days (format: L-T4-006)

Withdrawal does not apply retroactively to:
- Legally required record retention
- Data already subject to legal hold
- Audit logs (immutable by design)

## SECTION VI — DOMAIN-SPECIFIC LEGAL REQUIREMENTS

### VI.A — Voice GD Legal Requirements

**Absolute Legal Commitments:**
1. Platform guarantees in writing (L-T1-005) that NO audio is recorded, stored, or analyzed.
2. This guarantee is backed by quarterly technical audit. Audit results published in Transparency Report.
3. Voice GD session data is behavioral metadata ONLY.
4. Recruiters using Voice GD must sign a separate Recruiter Access Agreement confirming they will not attempt to infer audio content from behavioral scores.
5. Any technical modification to Voice GD that risks audio capture must go through a mandatory legal review before deployment. PR/commit blocked without legal sign-off.

**Recruiter Legal Notice:**
Recruiters must be explicitly notified:
- Scores are behavioral metrics only
- No audio recording occurred
- Scores may not be used as the sole basis for rejection
- Automated decision-making disclosure required if AI-assisted scoring influences hiring decisions

### VI.B — Innovation / Royalty Engine Legal Requirements

**IP Protection Framework:**
1. User submits idea → Platform creates time-stamped, hash-signed submission record (immutable)
2. Submission record = evidence of creation date for IP purposes
3. Platform's right of first refusal: 60 days from submission (must be stated in L-T1-001)
4. No idea content is shared with third parties without explicit, separately-signed Royalty Agreement
5. No idea content is used for AI model training without explicit written consent (separate from general platform consent)
6. Royalty payment schedule: quarterly, with 15-day dispute window per statement
7. Tax withholding obligations: Platform must withhold applicable TDS and issue Form 16A to users

**Minor Innovator Special Protections:**
- Ideas submitted by minors are locked from commercialization until age 18 or guardian consent
- Royalty payments to minors held in escrow until age 18 or guardian-authorized release
- Guardian may review all idea submissions and licensing proposals on behalf of minor

### VI.C — Dojo For Arts Legal Requirements

**Match Integrity:**
1. Score records are immutable after 48-hour audit window. Stated explicitly in L-T1-004.
2. Score override requires Mentor + Ops Console approval with full audit trail.
3. Belt certification = verifiable credential. Revocation only for documented fraud, with 30-day appeal period.
4. Cross-tenant score isolation: Users in one institution's Dojo cannot access another institution's match records.

**Video Participation:**
1. Default posture: NO recording.
2. Explicit opt-in per session required for replay.
3. Replay retention: 90 days maximum, then permanent deletion.
4. SFU-only routing: video never touches backend storage unless replay consent granted.
5. Parental consent required for minor participants before first video session.

**Scoring Transparency:**
1. Scoring formula must be published in the Dojo Scoring Transparency Notice (sub-document of L-T1-004).
2. Any change to scoring formula requires 30-day advance notice and new version of L-T1-004.
3. Users may request a scoring explanation for any match within the 48-hour audit window.

### VI.D — Society / Franchise Model Legal Requirements

**Franchise Agreement (L-T1-002) Mandatory Provisions:**
1. Franchise territory definition (exclusive or non-exclusive)
2. Commission structure: exact percentages, calculation method, payment schedule
3. Coach/Coordinator subordination: franchisee is responsible for ensuring coaches comply with platform policies
4. Minimum activity requirements and consequences of non-performance
5. Termination clause: 30-day notice for performance issues; immediate termination for policy violation
6. Franchise termination grace period management via Temporal workflow engine (ref: Society Architecture)
7. IP ownership: All platform tools, algorithms, and content remain platform IP
8. Data ownership: All student/participant data collected through franchise operations is platform data, not franchisee data. Franchisee may not extract, sell, or transfer student data.

Per Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996.
Arbitral tribunal: single arbitrator for disputes < ₹5 lakh; three-arbitrator panel above.
Seat of arbitration: [City], India.
Language: English.

Only after arbitration award. Jurisdiction: courts of [City], India.

5. Platform may NOT disclose complainant identity to the complained-against entity.
6. False complaint policy: If complaint determined malicious, complainant account subject to suspension. Legal action reserved.

legal_documents (

  change_summary      TEXT NOT NULL,
  storage_path        TEXT NOT NULL,         -- MinIO path for this version

## SECTION X — CI/CD LEGAL COMPLIANCE GATES

### X.A — Pre-Deployment Legal Compliance Checklist

Every deployment to STAGING or PRODUCTION must pass this checklist. Failure = STOP DEPLOYMENT.

**Gate 1 — Document Completeness Check**
All Tier 0 documents exist, are in LIVE status, have a valid effective date, and have a next review date in the future.
Automated check: `legal_documents` table query.
Failure → STOP → REPORT MISSING_TIER0_DOCUMENT.

**Gate 2 — Domain-Specific Document Check**
If deploying a specific domain (Dojo, Innovation, Voice GD, Franchise, Campus Portal): all Tier 1 documents for that domain are in LIVE status.
Automated check per domain tag.
Failure → STOP → REPORT MISSING_TIER1_DOCUMENT_[DOMAIN].

**Gate 3 — Consent Mechanism Check**
All active Tier 0/1 documents have a corresponding consent collection mechanism wired in the application.
Automated check: `legal_document_presentations` table has entries for the document code.
Failure → STOP → REPORT MISSING_CONSENT_MECHANISM.

**Gate 4 — Minor Consent Protocol Check**
If platform processes minor data: all 8 steps of Minor Consent Protocol are coded and verifiable in the codebase.
Manual legal sign-off required per deployment to production.
Failure → STOP → DPO_ALERT.

**Gate 5 — Royalty Agreement Wiring Check**
If Innovation Engine is deployed: L-T1-001 is live and the consent gate is wired before idea submission is enabled.
Automated check: `feature_flags.innovation_engine` AND `legal_documents WHERE document_code = 'L-T1-001' AND status = 'LIVE'`.
Failure → STOP → REPORT INNOVATION_WITHOUT_LEGAL_GATE.

**Gate 6 — DPA Check for New Vendors**
Any new vendor added to infrastructure since last deployment must have a signed DPA in the system.
Manual check by Legal Officer required.
Failure → STOP → REPORT VENDOR_WITHOUT_DPA.

**Gate 7 — Breach Notification Template Check**
L-T4-001 and L-T4-002 templates exist, are current, and are wired to incident response workflow.
Automated check.
Failure → STOP → REPORT MISSING_BREACH_TEMPLATE.

**Gate 8 — Document Version Hash Check**
All LIVE documents have a hash_signature in `legal_document_versions`. Hash verified against MinIO stored content.
Automated check.
Failure → STOP → REPORT DOCUMENT_INTEGRITY_FAILURE.

**Gate 9 — Grievance Officer Published Check**
Grievance Officer contact information is populated and live on the platform's public contact page.
Automated check: `platform_config.grievance_officer_name` and `platform_config.grievance_officer_email` populated.
Failure → STOP → REPORT MISSING_GRIEVANCE_OFFICER.

**Gate 10 — Minor Data Flag Check**
If any feature uses `user.is_minor = TRUE` scoped data, legal gate for minor processing is active.
Automated check.
Failure → STOP → REPORT MINOR_DATA_WITHOUT_LEGAL_GATE.

## SECTION XI — LEGAL DOCUMENT PRESENTATION RULES

### XI.A — User Interface Requirements for Legal Documents

1. **Visibility**: Legal documents must be accessible from the footer of every page and from the user's account settings at all times.
2. **Versioning Display**: The version number and effective date of the current document must be visible on the document page.
3. **Change Notification**: When a Tier 0 document is updated, an in-app notification must be shown at the user's next login with: what changed, a link to the new document, and the acceptance mechanism.
4. **Scroll-to-Accept**: For Tier 0 documents, the accept button must not be clickable until the user has scrolled to the end of the document (or equivalent mechanism confirming review).
5. **Print/Download**: Users must be able to download or print the current version of any Tier 0/1 document that applies to them.
6. **Language**: Documents must be available in English. Translations into regional languages (Hindi, Marathi at minimum) must be provided for Tier 0 documents before expanding user base to those regions. Translations must be legally reviewed. In case of conflict, English version governs.
7. **Accessibility**: Documents must be accessible per WCAG 2.1 AA standards (reference R20).

### XI.B — Consent UI Requirements

1. Consent checkboxes must have equal visual weight — no dark patterns.
2. "Accept" and "Decline" buttons must be the same size and prominence.
3. Declining must not result in hostile or confusing UI outcomes.
4. If declining blocks access to a feature: the reason must be clearly stated ("This feature requires your agreement to the Royalty Terms to activate.").
5. Consent must be a separate, specific action per document — no blanket agree-to-everything checkboxes for multiple documents.
6. For minor consent: guardian's acceptance flow must be on a separate, dedicated screen.

5. Legal Hold overrides all retention schedules
6. Legal Hold lifted only by Legal Officer via Ops Console with documented reason
7. All Legal Hold activations and lifts are written to immutable audit log
