## 📊 PART 2: CONDUCT RESTRICTIONS (SEALED)

### 2.1 PROHIBITED CONDUCT (HARD LOCKS)

```yaml
CATEGORY_1_HORIZONTAL_AGREEMENTS:
  DEFINITION: Arrangements between competitors at same market level
  
  PROHIBITED_CONDUCT:
    Price_Fixing:
      Description: Competitors agree on pricing, fees, commissions
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Let's charge 20% commission on jobs" (with competitors)
        - "All recruiter fees at $500 per hire" (agreed with competitors)
        - "Match competitor pricing" (coordinated with rival platforms)
      Enforcement: IMMEDIATE LOCKDOWN (PER SE ILLEGAL)
      Implementation: Pricing MUST be independent + unilateral
    
    Bid_Rigging:
      Description: Competitors agree on bids/offers in competitive context
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "We'll bid low for startup clients, you handle enterprise" (market allocation)
        - "Let's not bid against each other for India market" (geographic cartel)
        - "Agreed minimum price for freelancer services" (price floor collusion)
      Enforcement: IMMEDIATE LOCKDOWN
      Implementation: All bids independently determined
    
    Market_Allocation:
      Description: Competitors agree to divide markets/customers
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Antigravity covers job portal, Competitor covers skills training"
        - "India market = Antigravity, US market = Competitor"
        - "Enterprise clients = Antigravity, SME clients = Competitor"
        - "Bangalore jobs = Antigravity, Delhi jobs = Competitor"
      Enforcement: IMMEDIATE LOCKDOWN (PER SE ILLEGAL)
      Implementation: Cannot exclude competitors from any segment
    
    Customer_Allocation:
      Description: Dividing specific customers/accounts among competitors
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Google always posts jobs on Antigravity, Meta on Competitor"
        - "IIT students → Antigravity, BITS students → Competitor"
        - "TCS hiring → Antigravity, Infosys hiring → Competitor"
      Enforcement: IMMEDIATE LOCKDOWN
      Implementation: All customers can freely use all platforms
    
    Group_Boycotts:
      Description: Multiple competitors agreeing to exclude 3rd party
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Antigravity + Competitor refuse to list jobs from XYZ Corp"
        - "Joint refusal to work with freelancer John Doe"
        - "Coordinated policy to block India recruiter association"
      Enforcement: IMMEDIATE LOCKDOWN (IF ANTICOMPETITIVE)
      Implementation: Unilateral refusals acceptable, joint refusals = prohibited
    
    Output_Restrictions:
      Description: Competitors agree to limit supply/services
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Both cap jobs at 1000 per month to keep prices high"
        - "Agreed maximum number of freelancers accepted"
        - "Joint agreement to limit skill assessments per user"
      Enforcement: IMMEDIATE LOCKDOWN
      Implementation: No capacity restrictions coordinated with competitors
    
    Information_Exchange:
      Description: Competitors sharing competitively sensitive data
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Share pricing data monthly with Competitor"
        - "Exchange information on which companies we're targeting"
        - "Coordinate on recruiter commission rates" (reveals pricing strategy)
      Enforcement: RESTRICTED (context-dependent)
      Implementation: Public information OK, real-time competitive data = NO
    
  COMPLIANCE_MECHANISM:
    Communication_Protocol:
      - NO competitor communication on pricing, capacity, customers, strategies
      - NO trade association participation in pricing discussions
      - NO confidential data exchange with competitors
      - Business-to-business communication: Antitrust lawyer review required
    
    Monitoring:
      - Audit competitor communication quarterly
      - Flag any discussions of pricing, volume, customers
      - Log all third-party meetings (antitrust counsel present)
      - Document independent decision-making process

CATEGORY_2_VERTICAL_RESTRICTIONS:
  DEFINITION: Restrictions imposed on customers/suppliers at different market levels
  
  PROHIBITED_CONDUCT:
    Exclusive_Dealing:
      Description: Requiring customer to buy exclusively from you (forbid competitors)
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Employers must post ONLY on Antigravity, not on LinkedIn"
        - "Students must take training ONLY from Antigravity trainers"
        - "Institutions must use ONLY Antigravity's assessment system"
      Legal_Analysis: Per se illegal if substantial market power + effect on competition
      Exception: Partial exclusivity (50% requirement) may pass rule of reason
      Implementation: Customers must have choice
    
    Tying:
      Description: Conditioning sale of one product on purchase of another
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Buy job posting access → forced to buy premium analytics"
        - "Enroll in skill development → must take assessment ONLY on Antigravity"
        - "Use project execution → must use Antigravity insurance/payment"
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) market power in tying product + (2) appreciable effect on tied product market
      Implementation: Unbundle services, offer customers choice
    
    Resale_Price_Maintenance:
      Description: Dictating prices at which resellers must sell
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Trainers must charge ₹5000 per course minimum" (fixed minimum price)
        - "Mentor hourly rate must be ≥₹500"
        - "Recruiter placement fee must be 20% minimum"
      Legal_Analysis: Per se illegal under Sherman §1 (some exceptions: US rule of reason debate)
      Implementation: Suggest prices, don't enforce
    
    Territorial_Restrictions:
      Description: Restricting where resellers can sell geographically
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "You can only recruit from Bangalore region"
        - "Trainers can only serve Delhi NCR zone"
        - "Recruiters limited to IT industry only"
      Legal_Analysis: Per se under Sherman §1 (unless rule of reason justification: new entrant, etc.)
      Implementation: Customers can serve all geographic/industry segments
    
    Customer_Restrictions:
      Description: Restricting to whom resellers can sell
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Trainers limited to serve only students (not professionals)"
        - "This job can ONLY be filled by student tier, not professional tier"
        - "Mentor services limited to IIT students only"
      Legal_Analysis: Often per se if among horizontal competitors, rule of reason otherwise
      Implementation: Transparent, non-discriminatory access
    
    Most_Favored_Nation_Clauses:
      Description: Requiring customer to offer you best terms given to any competitor
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Trainers must offer Antigravity the lowest commission rate they give anyone"
        - "Employers: if you post cheaper on Indeed, must match price on Antigravity"
        - "MFN: If you sell to LinkedIn at better terms, must give Antigravity same terms"
      Legal_Analysis: May be illegal if prevent price competition + foreclosure of rivals
      Implementation: Avoid MFN clauses; allow customers independent pricing
    
    Bundling:
      Description: Selling products together as package (similar to tying, but bundle offered)
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Bundle: job posting + skill assessment + project execution (no unbundled option)"
        - "Premium tier includes all services, no a la carte option"
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if leverages market power + excludes competitors
      Implementation: Offer unbundled options at proportional pricing
  
  COMPLIANCE_MECHANISM:
    Contract_Review:
      - Audit all customer contracts for exclusivity, restrictions
      - Remove any territorial, customer, or price restrictions
      - Ensure unbundling option available
      - Document competitive justification (if any)
    
    Enforcement:
      - Unilateral refusal to deal (acceptable, generally lawful)
      - Cannot condition access on customer behavior in separate market

CATEGORY_3_UNILATERAL_CONDUCT_RESTRICTIONS:
  DEFINITION: Actions by dominant firm that abuse market power (if dominant)
  
  TRIGGER: Only applies if Antigravity market share > 50% OR has high barriers + control
  
  PROHIBITED_CONDUCT:
    Predatory_Pricing:
      Description: Pricing below cost to exclude competitors + create/maintain monopoly
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Jobs posted free (when cost = ₹50) to undercut LinkedIn (₹500)
        - Zero commission to trainers (while paying ₹1000 per trainer hourly)
        - Dumping pricing to destroy new entrant, then raise prices
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) below cost + (2) intent to exclude competitors + (3) likely to succeed
      Implementation: Price above variable costs; ensure margin positive
    
    Predatory_Exclusion:
      Description: Actions designed to foreclose competitor market access
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Exclusive dealing with major employers (forbid using LinkedIn)
        - Exclusive training partnerships (forbid external trainers)
        - Exclusive data access (competitors denied candidate database access)
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) forecloses substantial market portion + (2) likelihood of monopoly maintenance
      Implementation: Allow competitors access; don't foreclose market segments
    
    Essential_Facility_Denial:
      Description: Refusing to provide access to critical infrastructure
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Deny competitors API access to job database (if only comprehensive source)
        - Refuse data sharing with recruiters trying to compete on platform
        - Block competitors from accessing university placement records
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) facility essential + (2) network effects create barriers + (3) unreasonable refusal
      Defenses: (a) capacity constraints, (b) free rider concerns, (c) proprietary information protection
      Implementation: If dominant, may be required to license data/API (at reasonable rates)
    
    Bundling_by_Dominant_Firm:
      Description: Tying or bundling to leverage monopoly to adjacent markets
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - "Use job portal → forced to use Antigravity's skill assessment (tied market)"
        - Employer must use ALL services (jobs + assessment + analytics) as bundle
        - No option to buy job posting without analytics
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant in tying product + (2) forecloses substantial portion of tied market
      Implementation: Offer unbundled, separate pricing; document cost justification
    
    Exclusive_Dealing_by_Dominant_Firm:
      Description: Requiring customer exclusivity (dominant firm version)
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Require employers to post jobs ONLY on Antigravity
        - Require trainers to teach ONLY through Antigravity platform
        - Require institutions to use ONLY Antigravity assessments
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant firm + (2) substantial foreclosure + (3) no procompetitive justification
      Defenses: (a) free riding prevention, (b) quality control, (c) network effects
      Implementation: Cannot require exclusivity if dominant
    
    Predatory_or_Exclusionary_Conduct:
      Description: Conduct not price-based, designed to exclude rivals
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Deny competitor access to trained recruiters (no-hire agreements)
        - Interfere with competitor's supplier relationships
        - Exploit switching costs to lock customers in
        - Refusal to interoperate (jobs not searchable on other platforms)
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant + (2) likely to exclude rivals + (3) lack procompetitive justification
      Implementation: Transparent, pro-competitive policies; enable interoperability
    
    Discriminatory_Conduct:
      Description: Treating similarly situated customers differently
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Charge different fees to similar recruiters (without objective justification)
        - Provide different features to similar institutions (without cause)
        - Different job promotion visibility (pay-to-play that excludes poor companies)
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant + (2) discriminatory + (3) no cost or service justification
      Implementation: Transparent pricing; objective criteria for service differences
  
  COMPLIANCE_MECHANISM:
    Quarterly_Assessment:
      - Calculate market share (multiple market definitions)
      - If < 30%: Minimal restrictions apply
      - If 30-50%: Enhanced monitoring, avoid exclusionary conduct
      - If > 50%: Strict scrutiny, document procompetitive justification for any conduct

CATEGORY_4_INFORMATION_ASYMMETRY_PREVENTION:
  DEFINITION: Prevent abuse of information advantages in multi-sided marketplace
  
  PROHIBITED_CONDUCT:
    Preferential_Data_Access:
      Description: Giving platform's own services better data than competitors
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Antigravity training team gets real-time job posting data before external trainers
        - Antigravity recruiters see candidate applications before external recruiters
        - Internal analytics trained on full user data while selling limited version
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant firm + (2) forecloses competitors + (3) unnecessary for platform operation
      Implementation: Treat own services same as competitors; no information asymmetry
    
    Algorithm_Opacity_Abuse:
      Description: Using opaque algorithms to harm specific competitors
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Algorithm downranks jobs from competitor recruiters
        - Matches favor candidates from premium-tier employers only
        - Search results buried for certain training providers
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant + (2) intent to harm competitor + (3) no transparency/justification
      Implementation: Transparent algorithm; objective ranking criteria; no competitor discrimination
    
    Conduct_Based_on_Competitor_Data:
      Description: Using data about competitors to disadvantage them
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Monitor competitor trainer pricing, then undercut them
        - See which employers use Competitor, then target them with discounts
        - Track competitor recruiter placements, then hire their talent
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal if (1) dominant + (2) using data to exclude rivals + (3) foreclosure effect
      Implementation: Don't collect/use competitor data for anticompetitive purposes
    
    Self_Preferencing:
      Description: Favoring platform's own services over third parties (DMA issue)
      Examples_FORBIDDEN:
        - Antigravity's training service always shown first in results (self-preferencing)
        - Antigravity's jobs highlighted, trainer jobs buried
        - Platform owns recruiter? → rank their jobs higher
      Legal_Analysis: Illegal under EU DMA if gatekeeper; illegal under Sherman §2 if dominant + forecloses
      Implementation: Neutral ranking; treat all service providers equally
```

### 2.2 MERGER & ACQUISITION RESTRICTIONS

```yaml
MERGER_CONTROL_FRAMEWORK:
  TRIGGERING_EVENTS:
    Acquisition_Target:
      - Antigravity acquiring competitor in same market
      - Antigravity acquiring vertical partner (trainer network, assessment company)
      - Antigravity acquiring horizontal partner (job board, platform)
    
    Antigravity_as_Target:
      - Competitor acquiring Antigravity
      - Adjacent player (Google, Meta, LinkedIn) acquiring Antigravity
      - Private equity acquiring Antigravity
    
    Filing_Requirements:
      - US (HSR): If either party ≥ $111M (2024) + transaction > $111M
      - EU: If EU-dimension exists (turnover thresholds)
      - UK: If parties UK-relevant + turnover > £30M
      - India: If combination threshold met (asset value or revenue)
  
  MERGER_ANALYSIS_FRAMEWORK:
    Horizontal_Mergers (competitors):
      HHI_Test:
        - Calculate HHI before + after merger
        - HHI < 1500: Usually approved
        - HHI 1500-2500: Moderate scrutiny
        - HHI > 2500: Presumption of anticompetitive (unless defenses)
        - Increase > 250 points: Significant concern
      
      Competitive_Analysis:
        - Market share of merging parties
        - Competitive constraints post-merger
        - Entry barriers + likely entry
        - Efficiencies + consumer benefits
        - Vertical integration benefits
      
      Likely_Outcome_Examples:
        - Antigravity (30%) + Competitor2 (10%) = 40% → Likely challenged
        - Antigravity (15%) + Competitor2 (8%) = 23% → Likely approved
        - Antigravity (60%) + Competitor2 (5%) = 65% → Likely challenged
    
    Vertical_Mergers (supply chain):
      Conduct_Analysis:
        - Input foreclosure: Can merged firm foreclose competitors from inputs?
        - Customer foreclosure: Can merged firm foreclose competitors from customers?
        - Coordination: Does merger enable easier cartel enforcement?
      
      Likely_Outcome_Examples:
        - Antigravity (job portal) + TrainerNetwork (20% of training) → Likely approved
        - Antigravity (dominant job portal) + OnlyTrainer (60% of training) → Likely challenged
        - Antigravity + Minor Assessment Company → Likely approved
    
    Conglomerate_Mergers (unrelated markets):
      Concerns:
        - Tying leverage (use market power in one market to gain in another)
        - Vertical integration without direct input/output relationship
        - Ability to cross-leverage data
      
      Likely_Outcome_Examples:
        - Antigravity (jobs) + E-commerce Platform → Likely approved (no overlap)
        - Antigravity (jobs) + LinkedIn (dominant social + jobs) → Likely challenged
  
  COMPLIANCE_REQUIREMENTS:
    Pre_Acquisition:
      1. Antitrust impact assessment (internal, with counsel)
      2. Market share calculation (multiple definitions)
      3. HHI analysis (if horizontal)
      4. Competitive constraint analysis
      5. Determine filing threshold
      6. Prepare HSR/EU/CMA filing (if required)
      7. Phase I investigation defense preparation
    
    Filing_Preparation:
      - Engage antitrust counsel (4-6 weeks before filing)
      - Prepare detailed market definition memo
      - Compile competitive analysis, economic data
      - Prepare efficiency claims (if any)
      - Budget: $500K-$2M for major filings (US + EU)
    
    Remedies_if_Challenged:
      - Divestiture (sell part of combined firm)
      - Behavioral remedies (licensing data, open API)
      - Structural separation (fire-wall between divisions)
      - Pricing controls, transparency obligations
    
    Timeline:
      - Phase I investigation: 30 days (US), 25 days (EU), 20 days (UK)
      - Phase II investigation: 90+ days (US), 90 days (EU extended)
      - Decision: Block, approve, or conditional approval
      - Timeframe: 6-18 months for major transactions

ANTIGRAVITY_MERGER_RESTRICTIONS:
  Horizontal_Mergers:
    Size_Threshold: Cannot acquire competitor with >15% market share (same market)
    Rationale: Likely to trigger HSR Phase II + DOJ challenge
    Exception: With clear efficiencies (data benefits, cost savings) documented
  
  Vertical_Mergers:
    Restrictions: Cannot acquire supplier/customer if Antigravity > 40% + target > 30% in relevant market
    Example: Antigravity (40% job portal) cannot acquire major trainer network (60% training)
  
  Conglomerate_Mergers:
    Restrictions: Cannot acquire adjacent platform (LinkedIn, Indeed) if would create leveraging opportunity
```

### 2.3 EXEMPTIONS & SAFE HARBORS

```yaml
EXEMPTIONS_FROM_ANTITRUST:
  Foreign_Sovereign_Immunities_Act:
    Applies_To: Foreign governments, agencies acting as sovereigns
    Not_Applicable_To: Antigravity (private company, unless government ownership)
  
  Export_Trading_Company_Act:
    Applies_To: Export associations (export sales coordination)
    Not_Applicable_To: Antigravity (primarily domestic platform)
  
  State_Action_Doctrine:
    Applies_To: State governments, entities acting pursuant to state policy
    Not_Applicable_To: Antigravity (unless contracted by state entity)
  
  Labor_Exemption:
    Applies_To: Union activity, collective bargaining
    Relevant_If: Antigravity unionizes employees, negotiates collective agreements
    Careful: Cannot deny jobs to union members, cannot coordinate with employers
  
  Insurance_Regulated_Industry_Exemption:
    Applies_To: Insurance industry (some exemptions from Sherman Act)
    Not_Applicable_To: Antigravity (not insurance industry)
  
  Regulated_Utility_Exemption:
    Applies_To: FCC-regulated telecoms, FERC-regulated utilities
    Not_Applicable_To: Antigravity (not regulated utility)

      - Enforce minimum/maximum prices (illegal RPM)
```

### 5.2 SETTLEMENT NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK

```yaml
SETTLEMENT_DECISION_MATRIX:
  Cost_Benefit_Analysis:
    Fight_Option (Litigation):
      Costs:
        - Legal fees: $2-5M+ (long trial, expert witnesses)
        - Executive time: 50%+ diversion for 2-3 years
        - Uncertainty: Could lose (treble damages, injunction)
        - Timeline: 2-4 years to resolution
        - Reputational: Ongoing litigation damage
      
      Upside:
        - Avoid fine if win trial
        - Avoid consent decree restrictions
        - Avoid admission of liability
        - Establish legal precedent (if favorable)
      
      Probability:
        - Judgment: 30-40% win rate (if strong legal defense)
        - Appeal: Even if win, government can appeal
    
    Settle_Option (Consent Decree):
      Costs:
        - Fine: Likely ₹10-500 Cr depending on violation severity
        - Conduct restrictions: Behavioral remedies (pricing restrictions, open API, etc.)
        - Consent decree: 10+ years of regulatory obligations
        - Admission: Implied admission of violations (antitrust claim settlement)
        - Timeline: 6-12 months to settlement
      
      Upside:
        - Avoid treble damages (only pay actual damages in settlement)
        - Avoid injunction (likely worse than conduct restrictions)
        - Faster resolution (2-3 year savings)
        - Certainty (know penalty amount, can plan)
        - Reputational: Faster closure, smaller ongoing story
      
      Probability:
        - FTC likely offers 2-3 settlement options
        - Must accept within timeline or litigation resumed
    
    Settlement_Premium:
      - Savings = (Treble damages + legal fees savings) - (Consent decree penalty)
      - If win = 30%, expected trial cost = $3M + $50M treble damages
      - If settle = $5M fine + conduct restriction
      - Breakeven if settlement premium > Expected trial cost × (1 - win probability)
      - Example: 30% win, $3M to fight = Break even if settle for <$2.1M
  
  Settlement_Negotiation_Process:
    Phase_1_Initial_Offer (Weeks 1-4):
      - FTC sends proposed consent decree
      - Includes: Fine amount, conduct restrictions, compliance obligations
      - Antigravity response: Within 2-4 weeks
      - Counteroffer: If proposed terms unacceptable
    
    Phase_2_Negotiation (Weeks 5-12):
      - Back-and-forth: FTC clarifies concerns, Antigravity proposes modifications
      - Key_negotiation_points:
        - Fine amount (higher = more pain, lower = FTC savings face)
        - Conduct restrictions (broader = more intrusive)
        - Divestiture (asset sales required if integration already happened)
        - Monitoring period (longer = ongoing compliance burden)
      - Counsel strategy: Prioritize reducing fine, accept behavior restrictions
    
    Phase_3_Agreement (Week 12+):
      - Settlement agreement drafted (legal document)
      - Board approval required (major settlement)
      - FTC review + Commissioner vote (approval authority)
      - Public statement (simultaneous release to media)
      - Implementation (start compliance immediately)

SETTLEMENT_TERMS_NEGOTIATION:
  Fine_Amount:
    Factors_Determining:
      - Actual damages (consumer harm quantified)
      - Market power (dominant position = larger fine)
      - Duration (how long conduct occurred)
      - Severity (hardcore cartels = larger, unilateral = smaller)
      - Mitigating factors (early cooperation, confession)
    
    Typical_Ranges:
      Price Fixing Cartel: ₹100-500 Cr (illegal per se)
      Abuse of Dominance: ₹20-200 Cr (fact-dependent)
      Mergers: ₹10-100 Cr (if anticompetitive)
      Refusal to Deal: ₹5-50 Cr (fact-dependent)
    
    Negotiation_Levers:
      - Amnesty program cooperation (50-75% reduction)
      - Younger company (higher risk = steeper penalties to deter)
      - Recidivist (previous violations = higher fine)
      - Consumer harm quantification (if low = lower fine)
      - Antigravity leverage: Market position, customer value
  
  Conduct_Restrictions:
    Types_of_Remedies:
      Behavioral Remedy (preferred by Antigravity):
        - Example: "Antigravity must offer unbundled pricing options"
        - Duration: 10 years (typical for consent decrees)
        - Advantage: Avoids asset sale, maintains integration
        - Disadvantage: Ongoing compliance burden, regulatory monitoring
      
      Structural Remedy (preferred by authorities):
        - Example: "Antigravity must divest trainer network business"
        - Duration: One-time (asset sale)
        - Advantage: Removes competitive concern permanently
        - Disadvantage: Loss of revenue, integration unwound
      
      Hybrid Remedy:
        - Example: "Divest 50% of trainer network, comply with behavioral restrictions on remainder"
        - Often: Compromise position in negotiation
    
    Typical_Restrictions:
      - Pricing transparency: Report pricing quarterly to regulators
      - Most Favored Nation clauses: Banned (can't require customers give Antigravity best terms)
      - Exclusive dealing: Capped at [X]% of market/customers
      - Bundling: Must offer unbundled pricing (a la carte option)
      - Data access: Must provide competitors access to platform data (licensing)
      - API: Must maintain open API for third-party integrations
      - Non-discrimination: Must treat all competitors equally (algorithm transparency)
    
    Negotiation_Approach:
      - Concede behavioral restrictions (minor ongoing cost)
      - Resist divestiture (major one-time cost)
      - Push for time limits (10 years max, not indefinite)
      - Carve out proprietary technology (don't share core algorithms)
      - Request safe harbor for good-faith compliance (not minor deviations)
  
  Monitoring_&_Compliance:
    Compliance_Officer:
      - Antigravity appoints executive (compliance officer)
      - Reports: To FTC/DOJ + Antigravity board
      - Responsibility: Ensure ongoing compliance with decree
      - Independence: Reports to board, not to business executives
    
    Annual_Compliance_Report:
      - Certification: All behavioral requirements met
      - Documentation: Evidence of compliance
      - Issues: Identified risks, mitigation actions
      - Signoff: CEO + General Counsel + Compliance Officer
    
    Audit_Rights:
      - FTC can audit: Access to documents, testimony from employees
      - Third-party auditor: Antitrust counsel conducts annual audit
      - Access: FTC can examine any materials related to consent decree
      - Cooperation: Mandatory, non-compliance = contempt of court
    
    Compliance_Cost:
      - Annual compliance: ₹2-5 Cr (depending on restrictions)
      - Reporting: ₹50-100 Lakh per year
      - Audit: ₹50-100 Lakh per year
      - Ongoing legal counsel: ₹100-200 Lakh per year
      - Total: ~₹5-8 Cr annually for 10 years (post-settlement)

CONSENT_DECREE_TIMELINE:

  Settlement_Negotiation: 6-12 months (proposed decree to agreement)
  Comment_Period: 30 days (public comment on proposed settlement)
  FTC_Vote: 30 days (Commissioner review + vote)
```
