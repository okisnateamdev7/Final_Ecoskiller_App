## Purpose Declaration

The **Workforce Gap Model** is a sealed, production-grade enterprise intelligence module embedded inside ECOSKILLER's Enterprise Optimization and Trust Infrastructure layer.

Its singular mission is to answer the question every enterprise, institution, and talent ecosystem must answer continuously:

> **"What skills does our workforce lack today, what skills will we need tomorrow, and what is the precise, verified, actionable path to close every gap — at individual, team, department, and organizational scale?"**

The WGM achieves this by:

1. **Mapping** — Building a real-time, verified skill topology of every workforce entity using live data from ECOSKILLER assessments, Dojo matches, integration tool signals, and Digital Twin profiles
2. **Forecasting** — Predicting future skill demand using market intelligence, hiring plan data, project pipeline signals, and industry trajectory models
3. **Quantifying** — Computing gap magnitude, criticality, time-to-impact, and cost-of-inaction for every identified gap at every organizational level
4. **Prescribing** — Generating the minimum-effort, maximum-impact closure pathway per gap: hire, train, upskill, reskill, redeploy, or automate
5. **Tracking** — Monitoring gap closure velocity in real time as training, hiring, and deployment actions execute
6. **Trusting** — Ensuring every skill claim in the gap model is verified through ECOSKILLER's Dojo assessment system — not self-reported, not resume-derived

The WGM is the **operating intelligence layer** for enterprise workforce planning. It does not produce static reports. It produces a **living, self-updating gap map** that enterprises use to make hiring, training, and deployment decisions with verifiable data foundations.

# 🔒 SECTION WGM-H — GAP MAGNITUDE CALCULATOR

## Gap Computation Model

A **SkillGap** exists when verified workforce supply falls below forecasted demand within a given organizational scope and time horizon.

```
GAP MAGNITUDE FORMULA (LOCKED):

  current_supply = Σ verified_skill_level(user, skill) for users in scope
                   normalized to FTE equivalent (10.0 = 1 FTE-equivalent proficiency)

  projected_demand = DemandForecast.demand_quantity × horizon_weight

  gap_magnitude = max(0, projected_demand - current_supply)
  gap_magnitude_pct = (gap_magnitude / projected_demand) × 100

  horizon_weight:  H1: 1.00 · H2: 0.85 · H3: 0.70
```

## Gap Criticality Index (GCI) — Locked

```
DIMENSION 1 — Business Impact Score (BIS)    [weight: 35%]
  Input: role criticality tier × project dependency count × revenue attribution
  Scale: 0–10

DIMENSION 2 — Time Pressure Score (TPS)      [weight: 30%]
  Input: earliest demand trigger date vs current date (days)
  Scale: 0–10 (10 = gap creates risk within 30 days)

DIMENSION 3 — Closure Difficulty Score (CDS) [weight: 20%]
  Input: skill_scarcity_index + prerequisite_depth + avg_training_duration
  Scale: 0–10

DIMENSION 4 — Decay Risk Score (DRS)         [weight: 15%]
  Input: freshness_score decay trajectory + demand forecast slope
  Scale: 0–10

GCI = (BIS × 0.35) + (TPS × 0.30) + (CDS × 0.20) + (DRS × 0.15)
GCI range: 0.0 – 10.0

CRITICALITY TIERS (LOCKED):
  TIER 1 — CRITICAL:  GCI ≥ 8.0   (immediate action required)
  TIER 2 — HIGH:      GCI 6.0–7.9 (action within 30 days)
  TIER 3 — MEDIUM:    GCI 4.0–5.9 (action within 90 days)
  TIER 4 — LOW:       GCI 2.0–3.9 (plan within 180 days)
  TIER 5 — WATCH:     GCI < 2.0   (track only)
```

# 🔒 SECTION WGM-I — CLOSURE PATHWAY PRESCRIBER

## Closure Modality Framework (Locked — 6 Modalities)

```
MODALITY 1 — EXTERNAL HIRE
  Trigger: gap_magnitude > 2.0 FTE AND scarcity_index < 0.70
           OR time_pressure_score ≥ 9.0 AND training duration > 60 days
  Outputs: candidate profile spec, market salary band, sourcing urgency,
           ECOSKILLER marketplace search query auto-generated

MODALITY 2 — INTERNAL UPSKILL
  Trigger: internal candidates at verified_level ≥ 60% of target AND
           training_duration ≤ time pressure window
  Outputs: ranked internal candidates, Dojo skill track,
           training duration + milestone schedule, auto-enrollment trigger

MODALITY 3 — RESKILL (Career Pivot)
  Trigger: internal candidates with replacement_risk > 0.70 on current role AND
           prerequisites met for target skill within 90 days
  Outputs: reskill cohort, prerequisite chain pathway,
           timeline + investment, displacement protection plan

MODALITY 4 — INTERNAL REDEPLOY
  Trigger: skill available in another dept at verified_level ≥ target AND
           business impact in current role < business impact in gap role
  Outputs: candidate identification, redeployment business case, transition plan

MODALITY 5 — CONTRACTOR / FRACTIONAL
  Trigger: time_pressure_score ≥ 8.0 AND gap_magnitude < 0.5 FTE AND
           project-specific (not ongoing operational requirement)
  Outputs: fractional spec, ECOSKILLER marketplace search, engagement duration

MODALITY 6 — AUTOMATE / TOOL ADOPTION
  Trigger: skill.replacement_risk > 0.80 AND automation tooling exists in EIE catalogue
  Outputs: automation tool recommendation, integration complexity,
           workforce impact: roles affected + redeployment plan

MODALITY SELECTION RULE (LOCKED):
  OR-Tools constraint solver selects optimal modality mix.
  Constraints: budget_ceiling, time_ceiling, headcount_ceiling, retention_policy
  Objective: minimize(time_to_close × gap_criticality_index × cost_coefficient)
  Multiple modalities may be combined for large gaps (hire + upskill in parallel).
```

# 🔒 SECTION WGM-K — COST-OF-GAP ECONOMICS ENGINE

## Gap Cost Framework

```
COMPONENT 1 — Productivity Loss (PL)
  PL = gap_magnitude_FTE × avg_daily_value_per_FTE × exposure_days
  avg_daily_value_per_FTE = revenue_per_employee / 365

COMPONENT 2 — Recruitment Cost (RC)      [if HIRE modality]
  RC = (recruiter_fee_pct × salary_band_midpoint) + job_board_costs + interview_hours_cost
  Default recruiter fee: 15–20% of salary (tenant configurable)

COMPONENT 3 — Training Investment (TI)   [if UPSKILL / RESKILL modality]
  TI = (training_duration_days × opportunity_cost_per_day) + course_licensing_cost

COMPONENT 4 — Replacement Risk Cost (RRC)
  RRC = replacement_cost_multiplier × salary × attrition_probability
  replacement_cost_multiplier: 1.5–2.0× salary (industry standard)

COMPONENT 5 — Opportunity Cost (OC)
  OC = project_value_at_risk × gap_probability_of_project_failure

TOTAL COST OF GAP (LOCKED):
  total_gap_cost = PL + max(RC, TI) + RRC + OC
  (RC and TI mutually exclusive — max() selects dominant closure cost)

PAYBACK PERIOD:
  closure_investment = max(RC, TI)
  annual_productivity_restored = gap_magnitude_FTE × avg_daily_value_per_FTE × 250
  payback_period_months = (closure_investment / annual_productivity_restored) × 12
```
