# 3. Marketplace Trust Governance Across Environments

Strict environment separation is required for trust simulations and moderation testing.

---

## 3.1 Development (DEV)

**Purpose**

- Prototype trust workflows
- Simulate fraud, abuse, and policy scenarios
- Test content moderation algorithms

**Rules**

- Synthetic user, transaction, and content data only
- No live marketplace impact
- Debug-level logging for simulations
- Early-stage AI model testing sandboxed
- Workflow validation required before TEST promotion

---

## 3.2 Testing (TEST)

**Purpose**

- Validate fraud detection, moderation, and policy enforcement
- Test AI-based trust scoring

**Rules**

- Simulate transactions and content uploads
- Validate compliance rules & moderation logic
- KPI measurement for trust & safety effectiveness
- Logging level: INFO
- Multi-team review for validation

---

## 3.3 Staging (PRE-PRODUCTION)

**Purpose**

- Production-like trust and safety validation
- Executive review of dashboards & workflows

**Rules**

- Use anonymized production user & transaction data
- End-to-end moderation & fraud detection testing
- Policy compliance verification
- Logging level: WARN
- Executive review required

---

## 3.4 Production (PROD)

**Purpose**

- Live marketplace trust, moderation, and compliance

**Rules**

- Real-time transaction and content monitoring
- AI-driven fraud & abuse detection active
- Automated enforcement of marketplace policies
- KPI dashboards for executives
- Logging level: ERROR
- Audit-ready governance logs

# 11. Enforcement Policy

If any system violates:

- Marketplace policy enforcement rules
- Fraud detection or moderation standards
- Regulatory or legal obligations
- Trust & safety workflows

The MTE Agent must:

1. Freeze affected transactions or accounts
2. Trigger internal investigation & review
3. Initiate remediation workflow
4. Escalate to CRO/COO/CISO if unresolved
