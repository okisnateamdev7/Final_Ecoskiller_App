# 3. Business Continuity Governance Across Environments

Strict environment separation is required for continuity planning and testing.

---

## 3.1 Development (DEV)

**Purpose**

- Prototype continuity workflows
- Simulate process disruptions
- Test redundancy and fallback strategies

**Rules**

- Use synthetic or non-critical process data
- No live production impact
- Debug-level logging for simulations
- Workflow sandboxing mandatory
- Early-stage AI scenario modeling

---

## 3.2 Testing (TEST)

**Purpose**

- Validate business continuity processes
- Test redundancy & failover procedures

**Rules**

- Simulate interruptions using test data
- Validate continuity recovery timelines
- KPI measurement for recovery effectiveness
- Logging level: INFO
- Multi-team review for process validation

---

## 3.3 Staging (PRE-PRODUCTION)

**Purpose**

- Production-like continuity validation
- Executive review of crisis response workflows

**Rules**

- Use anonymized operational data
- End-to-end continuity scenario validation
- Cross-functional communication testing
- Logging level: WARN
- Executive review required

---

## 3.4 Production (PROD)

**Purpose**

- Live business continuity monitoring & incident readiness

**Rules**

- Real-time monitoring of critical processes
- AI-driven risk alerts active
- Integration with DR and crisis management systems
- KPI dashboards for executives
- Logging level: ERROR
- Regulatory compliance tracking
